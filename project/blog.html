ARDUINO

Arduino is an open-source electronics platform based on easy-to-use hardware and software. It's intended for anyone making interactive projects. The boards can read inputs - light on a sensor, a finger on a button, or a Twitter message - and turn it into an output - activating a motor, turning on an LED, or publishing something online. You can tell your board what to do by sending a set of instructions to the microcontroller on the board.

NVIDIA Jetson Nano

The NVIDIA Jetson Nano is a small, powerful computer designed for AI and machine learning applications. It's a compact development kit that brings the power of modern AI to embedded systems and edge devices. It's capable of running multiple neural networks in parallel, making it suitable for tasks like image recognition, object detection, and robotics. The Jetson Nano is a great platform for learning about and experimenting with AI at the edge.

Custom TPU Chip

A custom Tensor Processing Unit (TPU) chip is a specialized hardware accelerator designed for high-performance machine learning workloads. These chips are custom-built to optimize specific types of neural network operations, offering significantly improved speed and energy efficiency compared to general-purpose processors like CPUs or GPUs. They are typically used in large-scale AI deployments and are tailored for the unique needs of demanding machine learning applications, often focusing on inference tasks, and are designed to run trained models very quickly.

Espressif ESP32

The Espressif ESP32 is a low-cost, low-power microcontroller with integrated Wi-Fi and Bluetooth connectivity. It's a popular choice for IoT (Internet of Things) projects and embedded systems due to its versatile feature set and affordability. The ESP32 is capable of handling a wide range of tasks, from simple sensor readings to complex wireless communications. It's a great platform for building connected devices, smart home applications, and wearable technology.
